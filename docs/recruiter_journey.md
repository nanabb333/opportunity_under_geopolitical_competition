# Recruiter Journey

## Purpose

This document describes how a recruiter should move through the GitHub portfolio and what they should learn at each step.

## What to Read First

A recruiter should start with the GitHub profile README or master portfolio README. The first message should be simple:

> I build geopolitical risk analytics projects that connect research, data pipelines, scenario analysis, and decision-support interfaces.

After that, the recruiter should review the repositories in this order:

1. `opportunity_under_geopolitical_competition`
2. `Taiwan_Geopolitical_Risk_Project`
3. `AI_Geopolitical_Risk_Dashboard`

## Step 1: Research Foundation

Repository:

`opportunity_under_geopolitical_competition`

What they should learn:

- You can frame a complex research question.
- You understand geopolitical risk, industrial policy, and strategic sectors.
- You can build structured datasets from qualitative evidence.
- You know how to document limitations and avoid overstating results.
- You can convert dissertation work into reproducible analytics.

Recruiter takeaway:

You are not only making dashboards. You can reason from first principles, structure ambiguous problems, and maintain analytical discipline.

## Step 2: Analytics Engine

Repository:

`Taiwan_Geopolitical_Risk_Project`

What they should learn:

- You can focus broad geopolitical research on a specific regional risk problem.
- You can build an analytics pipeline around scenario or event logic.
- You can connect geopolitical events, coded indicators, and interpretable outputs.
- You can move from research framing into operational analytics.

Recruiter takeaway:

You can turn research into a working analytical engine.

## Step 3: Decision Support Product

Repository:

`AI_Geopolitical_Risk_Dashboard`

What they should learn:

- You can design a product-facing interface for complex evidence.
- You can communicate analytical outputs clearly.
- You can use AI/product framing responsibly.
- You understand the difference between decision support and prediction.

Recruiter takeaway:

You can make analytics usable for real stakeholders.

## Intended Progression

```text
Research credibility
        |
        v
Analytics implementation
        |
        v
Product communication
```

This progression positions the portfolio for roles in:

- business analytics
- geopolitical risk analysis
- data analytics
- analytics engineering
- AI product analytics
- research operations

## What the Recruiter Should Remember

The main portfolio story is:

> I can take an ambiguous geopolitical risk question, structure the evidence, build a reproducible analytics pipeline, and present the result as a decision-support product without overstating what the data can prove.
