# Research to Decision Support

## Project Evolution

```text
Dissertation Research
        ↓
Structured Dataset
        ↓
Historical Analogue Engine
        ↓
Observed Pathway Analysis
        ↓
Analyst Brief System
```

The project began as dissertation research on when geopolitical competition is interpreted as market risk or strategic opportunity. It then developed into a structured evidence base, a deterministic historical analogue engine, an observed pathway framework, and finally an analyst brief system.

This evolution keeps the original research framing intact while showing how academic evidence can be translated into a reproducible decision-support workflow.

## Research Skills

- Theory-driven research design
- Event-study framing
- Evidence coding
- Case comparison
- Transparent limitations
- Conservative interpretation

## Analytics Skills

- Structured dataset design
- Deterministic similarity scoring
- Scenario retrieval
- Pathway grouping
- JSON and CSV output management
- Reproducible script orchestration

## Decision-Support Skills

- Analyst-facing synthesis
- Evidence traceability
- Caveat design
- Dashboard communication
- Workflow documentation
- Separation between evidence, interpretation, and recommendation

## Boundaries

The project does not forecast geopolitical events or market behaviour. It does not assign probabilities, estimate expected returns, or provide investment advice. Its contribution is a transparent historical evidence workflow that can support structured scenario assessment.
