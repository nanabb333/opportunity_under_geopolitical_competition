# Event Monitoring Framework

## Purpose

This framework defines the event categories that should be monitored as the project evolves toward a continuously updated geopolitical intelligence system. Monitoring is designed for candidate discovery and evidence expansion, not forecasting or investment advice.

## Monitoring Categories

### Military Activity

Why it matters: Military exercises, deployments, or crisis activity can change perceived geopolitical pressure around strategic supply chains.

Coding priority: High when activity is unusual, officially announced, geographically relevant, or linked to Taiwan Strait and Indo-Pacific semiconductor risk.

Expected pathway relevance: Restriction pressure, supply-chain pressure, geopolitical-risk pathway.

### Semiconductor Policy

Why it matters: Semiconductor policy is central to the dissertation's state support and strategic importance mechanism.

Coding priority: High for official subsidies, production incentives, facility support, and national semiconductor strategies.

Expected pathway relevance: Named support pathway, broad policy support context, support/downside-offset pathway.

### Export Restrictions

Why it matters: Export controls directly shape market access, technology transfer, and firm exposure.

Coding priority: High when controls affect advanced computing, AI chips, semiconductor equipment, memory, foundry services, or strategic technology supply chains.

Expected pathway relevance: Restriction pressure pathway, restriction/substitution pathway.

### Industrial Subsidies

Why it matters: Subsidies, grants, loans, tax credits, and procurement commitments are core indicators of state support.

Coding priority: High when support is named, firm-specific, sector-specific, or tied to strategic capacity.

Expected pathway relevance: Named support pathway, broad policy support context, named support/downside-offset pathway.

### Sanctions

Why it matters: Sanctions can restrict financing, procurement, technology access, or cross-border commercial activity.

Coding priority: Medium-High when sanctions affect strategic firms, semiconductor entities, military-linked technology, or major supply-chain actors.

Expected pathway relevance: Restriction pressure pathway, entity-list pressure, substitution pathway.

### Supply Chain Relocation

Why it matters: Relocation announcements can indicate strategic reallocation, risk reduction, or policy-induced production shifts.

Coding priority: Medium-High when relocation is linked to state support, geopolitical risk, or critical manufacturing capacity.

Expected pathway relevance: Capacity reallocation pathway, support/downside-offset pathway, supply-chain pressure pathway.

### Diplomatic Meetings

Why it matters: Leadership meetings and diplomatic negotiations may change perceived policy direction or reduce/increase geopolitical pressure.

Coding priority: Medium when meetings produce concrete statements, agreements, restrictions, or support commitments; low when symbolic only.

Expected pathway relevance: Diplomatic shock, broad policy context, pressure relief, or pressure escalation.

### Election Events

Why it matters: Elections can alter expected policy support, restrictions, industrial strategy, or cross-strait risk interpretation.

Coding priority: Medium when election outcomes are directly linked to strategic-sector policy or geopolitical risk; low for ordinary electoral noise.

Expected pathway relevance: Policy uncertainty pathway, strategic-support expectation pathway, geopolitical-pressure pathway.

### Strategic Investments

Why it matters: Strategic investments can indicate capacity building, national-security prioritization, or cross-border supply-chain repositioning.

Coding priority: Medium-High when investments are official, firm-specific, sector-specific, or linked to industrial-policy goals.

Expected pathway relevance: Strategic investment pathway, named support pathway, capacity reallocation pathway.

## Review Standard

Monitoring categories identify candidates only. Every event still requires source verification, coding review, validation, and transparent evidence notes before entering the historical analog dataset.
