# Recruiter Summary

## One-Page Overview

This repository is a Geopolitical Intelligence Automation System. It turns dissertation research on geopolitical competition into a reproducible analytics product that retrieves historical analogues, identifies observed pathways, and generates structured analyst briefs.

It is designed for business analytics, risk analytics, strategy analytics, and geopolitical intelligence roles.

## What the Project Does

The system helps answer scenario questions with historical evidence. A user selects or reviews a geopolitical scenario, the engine retrieves comparable historical events, the transparency layer explains why each analogue was selected, and the brief generator produces a structured analyst-facing summary.

The output supports analyst review. It is not a forecast, trading signal, or investment recommendation.

## Why It Matters

Organisations facing geopolitical risk need disciplined ways to compare events without relying only on narrative judgement. This project shows how to structure uncertain political and policy information into a transparent analytics workflow.

The project demonstrates how to:

- convert research into a dataset;
- code qualitative evidence consistently;
- build deterministic retrieval logic;
- explain why results were produced;
- communicate limitations clearly;
- present outputs in a dashboard designed for analyst workflows.

## What It Is Not

This is not a forecasting system. It does not assign probabilities, estimate returns, recommend trades, or provide investment advice.

The product is best understood as an evidence-to-decision workflow: it organises historical information, retrieves comparable cases, explains similarity and divergence, and presents structured evidence for analyst judgement.
