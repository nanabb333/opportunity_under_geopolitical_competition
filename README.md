# Opportunity Under Geopolitical Competition

Research portfolio on when geopolitical competition creates market opportunity for strategically important firms and sectors.

## Research Question

When does geopolitical competition generate positive market reactions for strategically important firms or sectors through expected state support?

## Puzzle

Geopolitical competition is usually treated as a source of risk: uncertainty, restrictions, supply-chain disruption, and market-access loss. Yet some firms and sectors may gain when states respond with subsidies, procurement, protection, financing, or industrial-policy commitments. The puzzle is why markets sometimes price geopolitical pressure as opportunity for selected actors rather than only as aggregate risk.

## Theory

The core mechanism is state-backed opportunity. Geopolitical competition increases the strategic importance of some firms, sectors, technologies, and locations. When governments provide credible support to those strategic actors, investors may expect improved cash flows, protected demand, lower capital costs, or reduced downside risk. Positive reactions should be strongest when support is credible, material, and attached to identifiable public firms or sectors.

## Research Design

The current design uses a focused event-study approach in semiconductors and semiconductor-linked strategic industrial policy. It compares market reactions for named beneficiaries, eligible sector peers, threat-exposed firms, and broad benchmarks around support and restriction events. Primary outcomes are event-day returns and cumulative abnormal returns around short windows.

## Current Status

The repository contains a frozen pilot sample, event and asset coding files, and a script for building event-return outputs. The pilot is designed to test whether events can be dated, coded, and linked to market assets before interpreting returns.

## Repository Structure

- `docs/`: frozen research design, protocol, theory, sample, and pilot documents.
- `data/`: event, asset, and event-asset link tables, plus local raw price inputs when available.
- `scripts/`: reproducible scripts for building market return and event-study files.
